About the Project

Dana-DroughtWatch is a machine learning-enhanced drought early warning system developed by Samuel Muturi Njeri, a final-year BSc. Geospatial Information Science and Remote Sensing student at Dedan Kimathi University of Technology.

The system integrates multi-sensor satellite data:

  • CHIRPS – for rainfall monitoring
  • MODIS NDVI – for vegetation health
  • SMAP – for surface soil moisture

A Combined Drought Index (CDI) is derived from SPI, VCI, and SMI. A Random Forest model trained on historical data (2010–2023) predicts drought severity classes (Normal, Moderate, Severe, Extreme) up to 3 months in advance with 89% accuracy.

Operationalized via an interactive dashboard, Dana-DroughtWatch empowers pastoralists, county governments, and NGOs to make informed decisions on livestock migration, water management, and aid distribution.
